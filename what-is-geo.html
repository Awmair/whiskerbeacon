The search your customers run has changed

For twenty years, finding a business meant typing a few words into Google and scanning a page of blue links. That habit is shifting fast. A growing share of people now ask an AI a full question in plain language and act on the short list it reads back.

The engine does not hand them ten links to compare. It hands them an answer, often with just two or three names in it. If your business is not one of them, there is no page two to scroll to. You simply never come up, and you never find out you were left off. That is the gap GEO closes.

The acronyms, in plain words

GEO, AEO, and the rest

This corner of marketing has invented a pile of initials. Most of them point at the same goal: be the source an AI trusts enough to quote.

GEO Generative Engine Optimization

Making your business easy for AI engines that write answers, rather than list links, to find and cite.

AEO Answer Engine Optimization

The same idea, aimed at tools that answer a question directly, like a chat assistant or an AI summary.

AI Overviews Google's AI summaries

The AI-written summary Google now places above the old blue links, citing a handful of sources.

Schema Structured data

The labelled, machine-readable record your site hands to engines: who you are, what you do, how you are rated.

Entity How AI knows you

Your business understood as a specific, known thing in a real place, rather than a string of words on a page.

llms.txt The AI guide file

A small plain text file that tells AI models how to read and describe your site.

The short version

What exactly is GEO?+
Generative Engine Optimization is making your business visible to AI systems, not just search engines. When a customer asks ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity for a recommendation in your category, GEO determines whether your business is named in that answer. It is a distinct channel from SEO and needs a different strategy.
Is GEO the same as SEO?+
No. SEO ranks your pages in Google's link list. GEO gets your business named inside the AI-generated answer, which is what most customers see first. Only about 45 percent of businesses that rank on Google also appear in AI answers, so strong SEO does not guarantee you show up in AI.
Do I need structured data?+
In almost every case, yes. Schema is the machine-readable record of your business, services, location and rating. Without it, AI engines are guessing what your site is about, and they tend to quote sources they do not have to guess about.
How long before I see results?+

How do I know it is working?+
